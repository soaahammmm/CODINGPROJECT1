01THE THRESHOLD

Every house draws a line. On one side, weather is something you watch. On the other, it is something you are standing in. For four hundred years, Japanese carpenters kept a strip of floor that refused to choose — the engawa, a veranda under the eaves, belonging to neither the room nor the garden.

EN‑01 is that strip of floor, rebuilt as an entire dwelling. One room. One glass wall facing whatever is out there. Charred cedar on the other three sides, and open ground passing underneath.

It does not hold the wilderness back.
It holds you, near it.
